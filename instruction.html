Overview

Welcome to the Disaster Management Website. Here, you'll find essential information on how to prepare for and respond to various types of disasters.

What To Do During a Disaster

  • Stay calm and follow emergency instructions.
  • Evacuate if advised by authorities.
  • Have an emergency kit ready.
  • Stay informed through reliable sources.
  • Check in with family members to ensure they are safe.
  • Follow proper safety procedures for specific types of disasters.

What Not To Do During a Disaster

  • Do not panic or spread misinformation.
  • Avoid leaving your shelter unless necessary.
  • Do not use elevators during a disaster.
  • Do not ignore official warnings and advisories.
  • Do not approach damaged areas or structures.
  • Do not drive unless absolutely necessary.

Disaster Management Guide

Follow these steps to ensure your safety and the safety of others:

  1. Understand the types of disasters common in your area.
  2. Prepare an emergency plan and communicate it with your family.
  3. Ensure you have an emergency kit with necessary supplies.
  4. Stay informed about disaster risks and safety measures.
  5. Participate in community disaster preparedness activities.
  6. Regularly review and update your emergency plan.

First Aid Guidelines

Here are some essential first aid guidelines to follow in emergencies:

Basic First Aid Steps

  1. Assess the situation: Check for safety hazards before providing aid.
  2. Call for help: Contact emergency services if needed.
  3. Provide basic care: Administer first aid for common injuries such as cuts, burns, and sprains.
  4. CPR: Perform CPR if the person is unconscious and not breathing.
  5. Bleeding control: Apply pressure to wounds to stop bleeding.
  6. Burn treatment: Cool burns with running water and cover with a clean, dry cloth.
  7. Poisoning: Contact poison control and provide information about the substance.

Common First Aid Procedures

  • Choking: Perform Heimlich maneuver if someone is choking.
  • Fractures: Immobilize the injured area and seek medical attention.
  • Heat Stroke: Move the person to a cooler place, hydrate, and seek medical help.
  • Hypothermia: Warm the person gradually and seek medical assistance.
